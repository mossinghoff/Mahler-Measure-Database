Largest Mahler Measures of Littlewood Polynomials by Degree

Recall a Littlewood polynomial is one where all coefficients are ±1. The
question of maximizing the Mahler measure of a Littlewood polynomial,
relative to its degree, is known as Mahler's problem. This is related to a
number of flatness questions regarding this family of polynomials.

This list shows up to 25 of the largest (multiplicative) Mahler measures
of Littlewood polynomials of fixed degree d, for each degree up to 30. This
extends the list published in [2]. Both the Mahler measure M and the
normalized value M/√(d+1) are displayed for each entry.

Only one representative polynomial for each displayed value is shown,
always one where the coefficients of x^d and x^(d-1) are both 1.
