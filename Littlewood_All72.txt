Reciprocal Littlewood Polynomials with Small Measure

The 1487 reciprocal Littlewood polynomials with degree d at most 72,
(multiplicative) Mahler measure M in (1, 5/3), and first two coefficients
equal to +1. Listed values of the Mahler measure often occur more than once
(for the same degree, and across different degrees), since polynomials need
not be irreducible.

For each such polynomial f(x), let g(x^r) denote its noncyclotomic part,
with r a positive integer chosen as large as possible. Each entry in the
table shows the following:
  deg(f)  (deg(g):H(g))  M(f)  v  Coefficients(f)
A tag of * appears if the deflated noncyclotomic part g is reducible. Here,
H(g) denotes the height of g, M(f) is the measure and v denotes the number
of roots of g outside the unit circle.

References pertaining to small Mahler measures of reciprocal Littlewood
polynomials are listed below.
