Counts of Small Measures by Degree and Number of Roots Outside Unit Circle

Counts of primitive, irreducible, noncyclotomic polynomials with (multiplicative) Mahler measure less than 1.3 and degree at most 200
(counting only one of f(x) and f(-x)), and this total number broken down by number of roots outside the unit circle. The counts are
known to be complete through degree 44.
